[ WHO I AM ]

I've been writing backend code for ten years and I still find it genuinely interesting — which I think says something. There's a kind of satisfaction in systems that hold up under pressure, that don't surprise you at 2am, that the next engineer can actually read.

For the last few years I've been deep in digital identity — specifically the question of who gets to hold your credentials and under what terms. I work on Verifiable Credentials, OIDC4VP, and OID4CI: the plumbing that lets people prove things about themselves without handing over more than necessary. It's a space I care about beyond the technical side.

TypeScript most days. Rust when I need to be sure. I tend to distrust clever solutions and prefer ones I can explain out loud.
